CLINICAL HISTORY: 25 year old woman with a history of seizures for the last 4 years. Episodes were described as a sharp pain o the right side of the head, bilateral hand shaking and awareness for 2-4 minutes. Last seizure, June 24, 2009. 
MEDICATIONS: Dilantin. 
INTRODUCTION: Digital video EEG was performed in lab using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with 1 channel EKG. Awake through stage ..I sleep. Hyperventilation and photostimulation performed. 
DESCRIPTION OF THE RECORD: During wakefulness, there is a well organized posterior dominant alpha rhythm of 10.11 hertz. Drowsiness is seen with anterior spread of the alpha, POSTS and vertex waves. Patient enters stage II sleep with spindles and K complexes. During hyperventilation, there is slowing of the background. Photic stimulation causes driving and patient becomes drowsy with further stages of sleep seen. 
IMPRESSION: Normal EEG. 
CLINICAL CORRELATION: Normal EEG does not rule out diagnosis of epilepsy. If further concern for this diagnosis, repeat the study.
