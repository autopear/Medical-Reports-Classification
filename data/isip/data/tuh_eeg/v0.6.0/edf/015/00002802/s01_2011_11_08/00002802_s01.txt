HISTORY: 
66-year-old male presented on July 13, 2011, with tonic-clonic seizures at 2 a.m. Loaded with Dilantin, found to have 
hypertension, epilepsy, and gangrene of the right foot. 
MEDICINES: 
Dilantin, Ativan, amiodarone, others. 
INTRODUCTION: 
Digital video EEG is performed at the bedside in the ICU using standard 10-20 system of electrode placement with one 
channel of EKG. The patient is intubated. 
DESCRIPTION OF RECORD: 
Background EEG demonstrates a low voltage, continuous pattern with low voltage frontal theta. There is some slow 
rolling eye movements. Arousals are characterized by increasing muscle artifact. Towards with close to the records, stimulation produces a bit more artifacts. 
Heart rate 60 BPM. 
IMPRESSION: 
Markedly abnormal EEG due to: 
1. Marked background slowing. 
Paucity of normal epileptiform waves. 
CLINICAL CORRELATION: 
No epileptiform features were identified. The findings described above should be placed in the appropriate clinical context given the patient's severity of medical illness.
